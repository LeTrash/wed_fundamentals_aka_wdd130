Academic Advising

If you have questions about the school or your own academic record make an appointment with the Advising center! They can handle any questions you have with your academics and lead you to the right decision.

Room Reservation

When you need a quiet study room for yourself or a group you can easily reserve a free one on campus. Just sign in with your school account and type in the details of your session such as date, time, and specifc area. Be sure to book at least 2 days in advance to get your room! Although you may get lucky if you book only a few hours before.

Student Records

Having trouble with classes? Transcript problems? Make an appointment with the Student Records Center!

Academic Petition

Did a traumatic event occur to you during the semester? Were you unable to drop out of classes or inform your teachers of your situation? Submit an academic petition and you can get those W's cleared from your transcript.

Madison Library District

For someone who loves reading this place is a wonderful oasis from the universtiy. This lovely public library is free for all and you only need an I.D and proof of address in order to obtain a library card. They have a decent selection of books that range from several topics. They also have a lil bookstore with discounts and programs aimed toward various ages. You can also donate your own books to the store. There is also a printing service for a fee however their computers and wifi are completely free!

McKay Library

The campus library is a huge resource avaiable to all students and friends. There are circulation desks on each floor to clear up confusion and check out books. There are libirarins that specicilize in each department and are happy to help with research papers, sources, and access to materials. The Mab Lab on the first floor provides free access to adobe creative cloud, equipment for checkout, and 3D printing for only $0.10 a gram! On the 2nd floor there is also the Grandin Press which is a fully funcitonal repilic of the printing press that printed the Book of Mormon along side the Special COllections room that allow students access to primary resouce documents and prehistoric artificats. The second floor is also a quite area making it a wonderful place to study or complete assignments without disruption.

If none of these categorizes are what you're looking for then I suggest trying out the search bar on BYU-I's own website. I've used it a lot myself and it'll help you find several links!
