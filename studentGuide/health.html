Mental and Physical Health Resources

Click on the buttons to open the collapsible content.

Health Center

Located beside the Kimball and Hinkley building. There to aid with all your sickly matters! The office for BYU-I student insurance is also there. Feel free to walk in with any question at hand!

Wellness Center

Located on the first floor of the Hart building resides the Wellness center. They over several serivcies that are either free or below $30 such as a Wellness Assessment, Nutrition Consultaion, Fitness Consultation, Cholesterol Screening, an Inbody test, and Bioimpedance analysis. They also have programs such as Fit4Life and THRIVE: Mental Wellness.

Campus Recreation

Here you can search for programs that are either free or under $10.They have Fitness classes, Emotional health workshops, wellness workshops, more! The best thing to do is search around and explore what is available.

Counseling Center

Located on the top floor of the Health Center! There you can schedule an appointment to meet with one of their many counselors, free to students as long as they currently take 6 credits. They can also aid you with other health resources on campus such as meeting with a dietician or applying for Medicaid.

TAO

Therapy Assistance Online: An easy tool free to all students and those alike to educate and better themselves on mental health.

Campus Recreation

Here you can search for programs that are either free or under $10.They have Fitness classes, Emotional health workshops, wellness workshops, more! The best thing to do is search around and explore what is available.

Family Crisis Center

Located just by the Romance Theater on Main St. This lovely organization provides several resources for anyone in need. They provide resources for emotional needs and for escaping abuse.

Idaho Suicide Prevention hotline

Available 24/7, FREE, and confidental

Open to helping a friend, a loved one, or even yourself. All are welcome to use this hotline, regardless of suicidal tendencies .

If you're reading this, know that I'm proud of you and you should be as well. It's hard to keep youself in check, especially when you're handed control over your life that you didn't have before. It's not easy to accept help, it took me at least 6 years to finally talk to others. But it's worth it. This life is hard and you're not expected to do it alone. So I'm proud that you've made it this far and are taking the time to try, taking the time to see what you can do.
